Strategic Communication Plan

Strategic Mental Health Communication Plan Connor Buhr · PDF · Full document
Download PDF

A strategic communication plan developed for UAH's mental health services — aimed at increasing student awareness, utilization, and satisfaction with counseling and wellness programs.

01 — Situation Analysis

Where We Are

Survey data indicates UAH students are moderately to highly satisfied with their university experience (scores of 3–4 out of 5). Mental health service access emerged as a key driver of satisfaction — yet many students remain unaware of available offerings, and the majority have never sought counseling or wellness programs. Students expressed desire for faster service, expanded hours, better staffing, and improved marketing.

02 — Target Audience

Who We're Reaching

  • Undergraduate students (freshmen through seniors)
  • Graduate students balancing academics, work, and family
  • Commuter and resident students as secondary audiences
  • Faculty and staff
03 — Objectives

What We Want to Achieve

Awareness 75% of students aware of mental health services within one academic year
Utilization 20% increase in counseling & wellness usage over two semesters
Satisfaction Raise average student satisfaction to 4 out of 5 within one year
04 — Strategies

Our Approach

  • Consistent information dissemination across all channels to build awareness
  • Remove access barriers — easy scheduling and active outreach
  • Deliver excellent, student-centered service quality
05 — Tactics

Specific Actions

  • Social media campaign with mental health tips and service info
  • Posters in dorms and academic buildings; monthly email blasts
  • Mention counseling center in orientation packets
  • Online appointment scheduling portal
  • Evening appointments for commuters and working students
  • Wellness stations at strategic campus locations
  • Anonymous follow-up surveys; semester focus groups
06 — Key Messages

What We're Saying

  • Mental well-being matters — help is available at the counseling center
  • Your confidentiality is respected and protected
  • Asking for help is a strength, not a weakness
07 — Budget

Financial Overview

Digital promotion (Fall)$3,000
Print materials (Fall)$1,500
Orientation materials (Fall)$1,000
Pop-up events (Spring)$4,000
Technology improvements (Spring)$5,000
Surveys & assessments (Spring)$1,500
Projected Annual Total~$15,000
08 — Evaluation

Measuring Success

  • Monitor website visits, social media engagement, bookings, and event attendance
  • Post-year surveys on student awareness and satisfaction
  • Compare counseling usage stats to prior semesters for the 20% target
09 — Stewardship

Maintaining the Relationship

UAH will sustain trust with students through regular surveys, student advisory input, and transparent communication about service improvements. Demonstrating that student ideas are being put into practice is central to long-term relationship management.

The Situation

Amazon AI Dubbing of Banana Fish

When Amazon Prime Video released an AI-generated English dub of the beloved anime series Banana Fish in November 2025, the reaction was swift and fierce. Viewers immediately noticed the flat, robotic quality of the voices — and it didn't take long to confirm they were AI-generated, not performed by human actors. What followed became a case study in how brands can lose trust overnight — and how they attempt to win it back.

01

The Backlash

Clips of the AI voices spread rapidly across social media, sparking widespread outrage among anime fans and voice acting communities alike. Voice actors raised concerns about AI threatening their livelihoods and the integrity of the craft. Some viewers cancelled their Prime subscriptions in protest. The consensus: Amazon had shown a lack of respect for voice acting as an art form and for the dedicated fan communities that had championed the series for years.

02

Amazon's Position

Amazon Prime Video — one of the world's largest streaming platforms under Amazon.com Inc. — serves millions of subscribers globally. The company has long cultivated relationships with everyday viewers, content licensors, and creative talent. Yet the AI dub appeared without any public announcement or transparent rationale, blindsiding all three of those stakeholder groups simultaneously and creating a reputational crisis that demanded an immediate response.

Public Relations Response

How Amazon Responded

Amazon issued a public statement acknowledging the misstep and outlining corrective action. The AI-generated dubs were removed from Banana Fish and several other anime titles. The company committed to revisiting its AI usage policy and pledged that any future AI dubbing would be a collaborative effort with human performers.

"We recently tried out an AI dubbing feature on a couple of anime shows as a way to make things easier for viewers around the world to watch. We know this whole thing upset a lot of fans and worried people in the creative world. We're really sorry for the mix-up." — Amazon Prime Video Public Statement
